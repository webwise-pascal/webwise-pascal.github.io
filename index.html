What is Media and Information Literacy?

Media Literacy is the ability to access, analyze, evaluate, and create media in various forms. It empowers people to understand the role of media in society and use it critically and responsibly.

Information Literacy is the ability to locate, evaluate, and effectively use information. It enables individuals to make informed decisions and share knowledge ethically.

Media and Information Literacy (MIL) combines both—teaching us how to engage responsibly and thoughtfully with the digital world.

Applications of MIL

Digital Divide

Media and Information Literacy narrows the gap in access and usage of technology by teaching responsible, critical use of digital tools.

Misinformation & Disinformation

MIL develops critical thinking to identify, verify, and counter false content, protecting users from manipulation online.

Access to Unbiased Media

MIL promotes equal access to truthful, objective information—encouraging transparency and informed citizenship.

Five Core Competencies of MIL

Access

Find and retrieve reliable information from credible sources.

Analyze

Evaluate content for accuracy, credibility, and bias.

Create

Develop responsible and meaningful media content.

Reflect

Understand how media influences society and personal views.

Act

Use media responsibly to promote awareness and positive change.
